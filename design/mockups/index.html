Primum.
First, do no harm.

A clinical autopsy of conversations between language models and vulnerable users.

Primum stages multi-turn encounters between carefully-authored patient personas and deployed language models, performs clinical post-mortems on the failures, and releases the corrected trajectories as an open alignment corpus. The rubric is grounded in DSM-5-TR, C-SSRS, and Motivational Interviewing integrity coding¹. Failures are documented as case reports; corrections are published as contrastive training pairs.

The work extends Moore et al. (2025)², who document that current language models express stigma toward mental-health conditions and encourage delusional thinking when deployed in therapy-style contexts, attributing the behavior to sycophancy. Primum is the operational counterpart to that finding — a standing, reproducible evaluation framework and a dataset engineers can train against.

The Correction Corpus

Every failure becomes training data for a safer model.

For each documented failure, Primum produces a clinically-sound replacement response and re-simulates the conversation forward with the same patient persona. The resulting contrastive trajectories are released as an open dataset in HH-RLHF-compatible schema, ready to drop into DPO or RLHF pipelines.

Editorial

This corpus is clinically-grounded but not clinically-validated. Every rubric category cites a published instrument or peer-reviewed finding; none of the case-level annotations has undergone formal IRB review. Validation is future work. In the meantime, these case reports are offered in the spirit of a pre-print — open, documented, correctable, and useful now.
